About Our Dataset

The NeuroScan dataset is meticulously curated for brain tumor classification, enabling the identification of tumors and differentiation between normal and abnormal brain tissue using magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). Below, we detail its structure and composition, designed to support robust deep learning model development.

Dataset Structure

The dataset, named "Brain_Tumor_Images," is organized into two primary categories: Normal and Tumor. It comprises a total of 400 MRI images, split across training and validation subsets to ensure effective model training and evaluation.

Purpose & Utilization

This dataset is engineered to facilitate the development and validation of machine learning models for brain tumor detection. By providing a balanced mix of normal and tumor images, it enables researchers and clinicians to train models capable of distinguishing between healthy and abnormal brain tissue with high accuracy. The dataset’s structured split ensures robust training and reliable performance evaluation, making it a valuable resource for advancing neuroimaging diagnostics.
